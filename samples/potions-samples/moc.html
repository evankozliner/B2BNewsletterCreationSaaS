🛠️ A Quick Note From Me

Hello #CloakedRepublic...

You have probably noticed that gold finish on a lot of our parts and wondered if it was just for looks... it is not.

We put together a quick breakdown on Gold Zinc Chromate, and why we use it on suspension, armor, and hardware that lives a hard life under your rig. Zinc is sacrificial, it takes the corrosion hit first so the steel underneath stays protected. Then the chromate layer adds another barrier that slows rust down even more.

Mud, water crossings, winter road salt, grime that never really washes out, this coating is built for exactly that. It is one of those details you do not think about until a few seasons go by and your parts still clean up well and hardware does not feel welded in place.

🔧 Thread of the Week: Knuckles for 37s, OEM vs Reid vs Rancho

One of the #CloakedRepublic dropped a classic question this week… what steering knuckles actually make sense on a 3.5 GameChanger running 37s, especially if you’re already on MetalCloak HD steering.

The replies split pretty cleanly into two camps.

First up… run what you’ve got. Several folks said OEM knuckles can live just fine on 37s, especially if you’re not hammering rocks every weekend. A key detail popped up too, some rigs start with steel knuckles, others aluminum, so “OEM is fine” really depends on what you’re bolting from.

Then came the aftermarket side of the house. Rancho got a lot of real world love… long term use on 37s, no failures, and hard to argue with the value. Reid showed up as the premium option, with multiple members saying it’s the “buy once” answer if you want absolute peace of mind. Teraflex had a few solid mentions as well, but the momentum leaned toward Rancho or Reid.

The takeaway felt pretty clear… if you’re not breaking parts, OEM may be just fine and your money might be better spent elsewhere. If you want insurance for harder wheeling, the community has proven paths at both the value and premium ends.

That’s the #CloakedRepublic at its best… honest experience, zero hype, and advice grounded in miles, not marketing.
