Over Half of B2B Purchases Driven by Personal Need, Not Business Needs

What’s happening:
For the first time, personal decision drivers outweigh professional ones in B2B purchasing decisions, reflecting a shift from purely business-focused outcomes to personal alignment and emotional resonance.

Why it matters:
This evolution signals a need for greater creativity, storytelling, and emotional appeal in B2B marketing to meet buyers' personal priorities.

By the numbers:

Personal decision drivers rose from 35% in 2021 to 52% in 2024.

Professional drivers dropped from 65% in 2021 to 48% in 2024.

The bottom line:
B2B marketers should embrace this change to build trust, align with personal values, and create emotionally compelling content.

Evan's Take:

I've done near 50 interviews with small to medium size B2B companies over the past few months, and this finding sharply with what I've heard anecdotally.

AI is making decision makers care much more about personal connection (and personal advantages) they will get from interacting with your brand. React accordingly.

The big picture: Email deliverability is critical for ensuring your messages reach customers' inboxes instead of getting lost in spam.

Double Your Sales With Hand-Written Notes

The What: Handwritten thank-you notes—or high-quality photocopies—can double future spending. The study showing this focused on ecommerce deliveries, but the design of the experiment suggests the same findings apply in B2B as well. 

Source: Journal of Interactive Marketing (July 2022)

Study insights:
  • Customers receiving no note spent $25.97.
  • A photocopied handwritten note doubled spending to $52.07.
  • Original handwritten notes performed similarly to photocopies.
  • Machine-printed notes had no effect.
