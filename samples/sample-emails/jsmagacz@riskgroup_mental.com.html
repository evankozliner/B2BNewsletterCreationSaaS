Mental Health In The Workplace

World Health Organization: 5 Key Facts About Mental Health And Productivity

Good work environments help mental health, but poor ones—like those with discrimination or high stress—can make it worse. In 2019, 15% of working-age adults had a mental health disorder, and globally, depression and anxiety cost $1 trillion in lost productivity each year. Governments and employers can improve mental health at work by creating supportive environments, offering training, and ensuring workers with mental health conditions are included and protected.

A Framework for Protecting and Promoting Employee Mental Health through Supervisor Supportive Behaviors

Workers are facing high levels of stress, anxiety, and depression due to global issues like the COVID-19 pandemic and climate change. Supervisors can play a key role in protecting employee mental health by offering emotional support, recognizing warning signs, and reducing stigma at work. Studies show that strong social connections at work are as important to our health as quitting smoking, making supportive supervisors essential for a healthier workplace.
