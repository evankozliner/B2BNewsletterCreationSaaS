🖼 NY Art

Notable Photography Exhibitions Set for This Fall

Exhibitions in New York, Richmond, and D.C. this fall highlight the enduring significance and beauty of photography as a medium. The traveling exhibition "A Long Arc: Photography and the American South Since 1845," showcasing pivotal moments in American history such as the civil rights era, is set to open at the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts in Richmond. Other notable exhibitions include celebrations of Robert Frank's work and "The '70s Lens: Reimagining Documentary Photography," focusing on the evolution of documentary photography through diverse voices in the 1970s. Further enriching the landscape of photography, the exhibition "American, Born Hungary" will also present influential figures like Andre Kertesz and Robert Capa, offering a broader understanding of the medium's artistic potential.

House Reflects Memories of a Lost New York Community

The Federal-style building on Greenwich Street serves as a poignant reminder of a past era, standing in contrast to the contemporary luxury condos surrounding it. A notable feature is a 1984 text painting by conceptual artist Lawrence Weiner, which encapsulates themes of loss and utility, referencing water's journey from source to usage. The legacies of Buczak and Hendricks, who contributed to the house's unique story, are preserved despite their passing, creating a monument to a bygone time in New York City. The interior reflects their shared life, embodying a history that risks fading from collective memory.

Rembrandt Painting Discovered in Attic Sells for $1.4 Million in Maine

A 17th century painting titled "Portrait of a Girl" by Rembrandt was discovered in an attic in Camden, Maine, selling for $1.4 million at auction. Art appraiser Kaja Veilleux found the portrait amid various art pieces, with a label indicating it had been loaned to the Philadelphia Museum of Art for a 1970 exhibition. The painting, created on oak and housed in a hand-carved gold Dutch frame, sparked intense bidding on August 24, culminating in a sale to a European collector. Rembrandt, known for his diverse body of work, continues to captivate art collectors and enthusiasts alike.
